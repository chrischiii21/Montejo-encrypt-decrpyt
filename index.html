Why is encryption important?

Encryption helps protect sensitive data from unauthorized access. It ensures privacy, secures financial transactions, and is essential in cybersecurity.

Can encryption be misused?

Yes, encryption can be misused. For example, criminals use encrypted messaging apps to avoid law enforcement detection, and ransomware attackers encrypt victims' files to demand ransom.

Real-life ethical concerns about encryption:

  • Government Surveillance vs. Privacy: Governments argue that encryption hinders law enforcement in investigating crimes, while privacy advocates insist on strong encryption for protecting personal data (Source: Electronic Frontier Foundation).
  • Dark Web & Cybercrime: Criminal organizations use encryption to hide illegal activities, making it difficult for authorities to track them (Source: Europol Reports).
  • Corporate Responsibility: Tech companies face ethical dilemmas about providing encryption backdoors to governments while maintaining user trust (Source: The Guardian, Apple vs. FBI case).
